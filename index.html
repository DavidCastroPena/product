01 · Overview
How to talk to users

A framework from Gustaf Alströmer (YC Partner) on conducting user interviews that generate real insight — not false validation.

1
Find the right users — they'll keep you honestThe people who tell you hard truths are more valuable than those who tell you what you want to hear.
2
Face users directly — don't hide behind messagesThe key to user growth is showing up. No-reply emails kill learning.
3
Listen first, build secondYour job in these conversations is to understand problems, not pitch solutions.
02 · Getting in the room
Writing outreach that works

Gustaf's messages work because they lead with genuine connection, not a pitch. Study what makes them land.

Gustaf → CFO at Motion Inc
"Hi! This is Gustaf from Airbnb. We overlapped in 2014 when you were on the Finance team… I'm talking to people like yourself to learn as much as possible about how companies think about carbon emissions. Would you have time for a 20 min call?"
What works
  • Shared history / connection
  • Clear, bounded ask (20 min)
  • Framed as learning, not selling
  • Specific topic area
What doesn't
  • Introducing your product
  • Open-ended "pick your time"
  • Generic "I'd love to connect"
  • Sending from a no-reply
03 · The golden rules
Don't introduce your product
"Your job is to understand their world, not explain yours."
👂
Listen, don't talkAim for 20% speaking, 80% listening. Silence after a response often prompts the most honest answers.
🔓
Ask open-ended questions onlyYes/no questions shut conversations down. Open questions reveal mental models you didn't know existed.
📺
Ask them to screenshareWatching someone actually try to solve the problem reveals 10× more than what they say about it.
04 · The interview script
Questions that unlock insight
01Tell me how you do X today.
02What is the hardest thing about doing X?
03Why is it hard?
04How often do you have to do X?
05Why is it important for your company to do X?
Follow up every answer with: "What do you mean by that?"  ·  "Can you tell me more?"  ·  "Why is this important to you?"
05 · Traps to avoid
Questions that waste the call

Users have good problems but often bad solutions. They have no incentive to say no to a feature — so stop asking about features.

✗Will you use our product?
✗Which features would make it better?
✗Yes / no questions
✗How would a better product look?
✗Two questions at the same time
✗Hypotheticals about the future
"Users have great problems. They often have terrible solutions. Focus on the problem."
06 · After the conversation
Synthesize, then build
🗒️

Synthesize learnings

Document verbatim quotes and patterns across interviews

💡

Problem–solution hypothesis

Write one crisp sentence: "People with X problem need Y"

✏️

Sketch the MVP

Only the smallest thing that tests your hypothesis

Before building, validate the problem is worth solving:

GreenAre people paying money for solutions in this space today?
GreenHave users tried to solve this on their own?
SignalSome customers will always be easier to sell to — who?
07 · After the MVP
Observe, don't tell them what to do
👀

Watch them solve the problem

Don't explain the product. Let them use it. Where they get stuck is your roadmap.

💬

Prompt them to speak their mind

"Tell me what you're thinking right now." Silence is not allowed — narration reveals assumptions.

🔒

Create an exclusive channel

Make early users feel special. A private Slack, early access, a direct line to you — they give more when they feel invested.

🤝

Connect them with each other

Users in the same problem space love meeting each other. You become the hub — and learn even more from the conversation.

08 · Real example
Carbon emissions — YC case study

Gustaf's actual discovery questions for a potential carbon emissions startup. Notice: no product mention anywhere.

→Do you care about carbon emissions at your company? Why or why not?
→Who inside your company would care about this problem most?
→How are you currently measuring or tracking emissions?
→What's the hardest part of your current process?
→Why is this important for your company specifically?
